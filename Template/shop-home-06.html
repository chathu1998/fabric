About Knit Fabric

Knit fabric is a type of textile material created by interlocking loops of yarn, forming a series of interconnected rows. Unlike woven fabric, which is created by interlacing yarns at right angles, knit fabric is made by continuously looping yarns together. This looping creates a flexible and stretchable fabric with a unique texture. Knit fabrics offer excellent elasticity, allowing them to stretch and recover their shape. They are comfortable to wear and provide a close fit to the body. Knit fabrics can be made from various types of yarns, such as cotton, wool, polyester, or blends, resulting in different characteristics and properties. They are commonly used in garments like sweaters, t-shirts, socks, and activewear, as well as in other applications like blankets and upholstery. Knit fabrics are valued for their comfort, stretchability, and versatility in creating garments and textiles.

Knit Fabric Types

Exploring Common Knit Fabric Types

Jersey Knit

Jersey is a lightweight, single-knit fabric with a smooth and flat surface. It has a good amount of stretch and is commonly used for T-shirts, dresses, and casual garments.

Interlock Knit

Interlock knit is a double-knit fabric with two layers of stitches that create a smooth and stable fabric. It has a higher degree of stretch and recovery compared to jersey knit, making it suitable for fitted garments.

Rib Knit

Rib knit fabrics have raised vertical lines or ribs alternating with recessed rows. They are often used for cuffs, collars, and waistbands, as the ribbing provides elasticity and a snug fit.

French Terry

French Terry is a knit fabric with loops on one side and a smooth surface on the other. It is soft, absorbent, and comfortable, making it ideal for sweatshirts, loungewear, and athleisure garments.

Ponte Knit

Ponte knit is a heavier and more stable double-knit fabric with a firm texture. It has a good amount of stretch and recovery and is often used for structured garments like skirts, pants, and jackets.

Jacquard Knit

Jacquard knit fabrics feature intricate patterns or designs that are knitted into the fabric using a jacquard mechanism. They can range from simple patterns to complex motifs and are often used for stylish sweaters and dresses.

Brush Back

Brush back in knit refers to a type of fabric where one side is brushed to create a soft, fuzzy texture, while the other side remains smooth. It is often used in sweatshirts and activewear for its comfortable and cozy feel against the skin.
